Passion Projects
One of the first projects I undertook, back in late 2019 as soon as I wrote my first python code, was making a Dungeons and Dragons level 1 character randomizer. For those of you who do not know what this entails, to play DnD, you need a character to play as because DnD is a role playing game. To make a character, you typically have to have to pick your race, class, background, starting equipment (weapons, armor, etc.), and spells if applicable, at level 1. You also need to determine your character's statistics or stats for short. A few other things as well, but those are the main points. In short, this project randomizes all possible choices one could make when building a level one character.

Going back to my enjoyment of numbers, my next personal project I undertook was creating a Sudoku generator. One day after thinking about it a lot, I felt like doing the math out and came up with what I now believe is a version of a backstep algorithm that makes a complete Sudoku solution. Afterwards, I decided to just fully make a random Sudoku puzzle generator. To make the Sudoku puzzle from the solution, I take a bunch of random clues out of the solution, and then see if the puzzle has no more than one unique solution. When I find a configuration of clues that has the one solution, it is then presented to the user for solving.
